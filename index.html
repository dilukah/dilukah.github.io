Robotic Systems Development

Automatic manipulation of non-magnetic micro particles on air-ferrofluid interface

The majority of natural materials are not magnetic. Most magnetic manipulation methods, however, are confined to manipulating magnetic materials. Here we utilize the deformation of an air-magnetic liquid interface for manipulation of non magnetic micro particles. The control algorithms and software developed can manipulate non magnetic particle in a pre defined trajectory with a path following accuracy up to 25 µm. The figure shows the path following for the letters of "Aalto" on ferrofluid. The scale bar is 2 mm.

Biomaterial tensile tester with real-time force and vison feedback

A micromanipulation setup developed for Gel-like material testing. The force feedback is used for understanding how fiber quality parameters such as toughness or stiffness change when the fibers are fabricated with different pulling velocity or lengths.

Tele-rehabilitation with Dynamic Gravity Compensation

This robot system is intended to provide remote physiotherapy to patients in rural areas. The therapist will still be able to feel the patient's abnormalities using the robot. An automatic weight estimation algorithm was developed to estimate the weight of the patient's arm weight within the first six seconds of the exercise. The weight of the patient's arm is then compensated for by the robot, and the therapist will only feel the abnormalities without the weight. The system effectively reduces fatigue and enhances performance.
